# Category D — Practical Digital Skills

This category covers real digital tools students will use for business. Every student task in this category must include exact, step-by-step phone instructions. Assume the student has never used the tool before.

---

## D1: Creating a professional email and using it

**Student task type:** Step-by-step guided task  
**Interaction pattern:** Student creates a real Gmail account and sends a practice email

**Local grounding hooks:**
- Inkify sends quotes and invoices by email to customers
- Job applications in the area increasingly require an email address
- Teachers themselves use email — frame this as a tool students are ready for

**Hard rules:**
- Include every sub-step (Play Store → Install → Create account → choose address → password)
- Explain the subject line — many first-time email users have never seen one
- Warn about unprofessional email addresses — this is a real barrier students face

## D2: Making a simple flyer in Canva or PowerPoint

**Student task type:** Step-by-step guided task  
**Interaction pattern:** Student creates a real flyer for a product or service

**Local grounding hooks:**
- Canva is free, works on Android, and does not require design knowledge
- Inkify prints flyers — a completed Canva design could become a real printed flyer
- Local WhatsApp groups as the primary distribution channel for flyers

**Hard rules:**
- Use Canva by default (free, mobile-friendly); mention PowerPoint as the alternative for school PCs
- Student task must result in a saved or downloaded flyer — not just a draft
- Include the step to share via WhatsApp at the end

## D3: Using Excel to track stock/inventory

**Student task type:** Step-by-step guided task  
**Interaction pattern:** Student creates a simple spreadsheet with 5 real products/items

**Local grounding hooks:**
- Google Sheets is the accessible alternative to Excel — free, works in a browser
- Dolly Dlezi (accountant at Msenti Hub) uses spreadsheets for small business bookkeeping
- Spaza shop inventory as the practical example

**Hard rules:**
- Use Google Sheets as the primary tool (free, no installation required)
- Keep it to 3 columns maximum: Item name, Quantity, Price
- Do not introduce formulas in this first lesson — manual tracking is enough

## D4: Writing a quote/invoice for a customer

**Student task type:** Write and reflect  
**Interaction pattern:** Student writes a real quote for a hypothetical or real customer

**Local grounding hooks:**
- Inkify creates quotes for printing customers
- Hlobisile Pearl Studios quotes for photography events
- MTN Mobile Money or Capitec as the payment methods listed on the invoice

**Hard rules:**
- Provide a simple invoice template with 5 fields: Your name, Date, Customer name, What you are charging for, Total amount
- Include the payment method — students should know where to ask for payment
- Do not use the word "invoice" without explaining it — "a written record of what someone owes you" is clearer

## D5: Using Google Maps to plan a delivery route

**Student task type:** Step-by-step guided task  
**Interaction pattern:** Student uses Google Maps to find a route between two real places they know

**Local grounding hooks:**
- Delivery as a zero-capital business (no stock needed, uses existing roads)
- Local context: Port Shepstone area, nearby towns, Msenti Hub location
- WhatsApp as the tool for coordinating deliveries with customers

**Hard rules:**
- Only introduce the directions/routing feature — not all features of Maps
- Student task must use a real destination they know (school → home, or home → Msenti Hub)
- Connect to business: "If a customer 3km away orders from you, this is how you plan the trip"
