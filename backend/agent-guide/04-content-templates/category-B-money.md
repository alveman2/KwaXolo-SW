# Category B — Money & Budgeting

---

## B1: Creating a weekly budget for your venture

**Student task type:** Write and reflect  
**Interaction pattern:** Student fills in a simple budget template for a real or hypothetical business

**Local grounding hooks:**
- Dolly Dlezi (accountant at Msenti Hub) helps local businesses with basic bookkeeping
- Weekly budget frame: students think in weeks, not months
- Cash-dominant economy — budget in cash, not bank transfers

**Hard rules:**
- Budget must have exactly 3 rows: Money coming in / Money going out / What is left
- Do not use the word "budget" without defining it: "a plan for your money — what comes in and what goes out"
- Keep it to weekly numbers — monthly feels too abstract

## B2: Tracking income and expenses in a spreadsheet

**Student task type:** Step-by-step guided task  
**Interaction pattern:** Student creates a Google Sheets spreadsheet with 5 real or sample entries

**Local grounding hooks:**
- Google Sheets is free and works on any phone or PC
- Dolly Dlezi uses spreadsheets for Msenti Hub clients
- Example: 1LT Bakery tracks ingredient costs vs bread sales income

**Hard rules:**
- Use Google Sheets, not Excel (free, browser-based, works on phones)
- Only 3 columns: Date / Description / Amount (+ or -)
- Do not introduce formulas — manual tracking is the goal for this lesson
- Explain the difference between income (money in) and expense (money out) in plain language

## B3: Understanding profit, revenue, and cost

**Student task type:** Write and reflect  
**Interaction pattern:** Student calculates a simple example using their own numbers

**Local grounding hooks:**
- Use food business as the primary example (baking, snacks) — most relatable zero-capital option
- 1LT Bakery as a named example
- Frame profit as "what is left for you" — not a technical definition

**Hard rules:**
- Define all three terms in plain language before using them:
  - Revenue = all the money customers paid you
  - Cost = all the money you spent to make the product or deliver the service
  - Profit = revenue minus cost = what is left for you
- Student task must involve real numbers the student chooses — not fixed textbook numbers
- Never use symbols like R (Rand) without writing "Rand" in full on first use

## B4: Opening a business bank account (or using MTN MoMo)

**Student task type:** Explore and observe  
**Interaction pattern:** Student researches Capitec or MTN Mobile Money requirements on their phone

**Local grounding hooks:**
- Capitec Bank is the most accessible — low minimum balance, available in Port Shepstone area
- MTN Mobile Money (MoMo) as the zero-bank alternative — only requires a phone and ID
- Msenti Hub can provide a letter of introduction for SEDA registration if needed for account opening

**Hard rules:**
- Present both options: bank account (Capitec) and mobile money (MTN MoMo) — not everyone has ID documents
- Do not assume the student is over 18 — flag which options are available to minors
- Make clear this lesson is about knowing the options — not about opening an account today

## B5: Applying for a micro-loan at Msenti Hub

**Student task type:** Write and reflect  
**Interaction pattern:** Student writes a short explanation of what they would use a loan for

**Local grounding hooks:**
- Msenti Hub directly — Victor Jaca CEO, offers micro-finance support
- SEDA Port Shepstone — also offers some funding pathways
- Frame loans as a last resort, not a first step — "only borrow what you can repay with the money the business earns"

**Hard rules:**
- Never make a loan sound easy or risk-free
- Explain interest in plain language: "If you borrow R500 and the interest is 10%, you pay back R550 — the extra R50 is the cost of borrowing"
- Student task focuses on: what would I use the money for, and how would I pay it back — not the application form itself
