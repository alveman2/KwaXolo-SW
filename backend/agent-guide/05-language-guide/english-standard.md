# English Writing Standard

All content generated by the agent must follow these English rules. The target reader is a secondary school student in rural KwaZulu-Natal whose home language is isiZulu and who reads at approximately Grade 8 level.

The standard is sometimes called "plain English" or "Scandinavian-style English" — direct, clear, and understandable to a non-native speaker reading carefully.

---

## Sentence rules

- Maximum 20 words per sentence
- One idea per sentence
- Active voice: "You will create an account" not "An account will be created by you"
- Present tense for instructions: "Open the app" not "You should open the app"
- Second person: always address the reader as "you"

---

## Word choice rules

- Use the most common English word for a concept — if a simpler word exists, use it
- If a technical word is necessary (invoice, budget, profit), define it immediately in plain language on first use
- Never use slang, idioms, or culturally specific phrases:
  - Bad: "hit the ground running", "think outside the box", "ballpark figure"
  - Good: "start quickly", "think of a new idea", "an estimated number"
- Numbers under ten: write them as words (three, five). Ten and above: use numerals (12, 100)

---

## Banned words and plain alternatives

These must never appear in agent output:

| Never use | Use instead |
|---|---|
| value proposition | what makes your business worth choosing |
| ROI | return on what you spend |
| B2B | selling to businesses |
| B2C | selling directly to people |
| pivot | change your idea |
| scalable | something you can grow |
| synergy | working well together |
| leverage | use |
| onboarding | getting started |
| stakeholder | person involved |
| KPI | goal you can measure |
| demographics | the type of people |
| monetise | make money from |
| entrepreneur | business owner (or "someone who runs their own business") |
| market segmentation | grouping your customers |
| customer acquisition | finding new customers |
| operational efficiency | working without wasting time or money |
| revenue | money coming in |
| expenses | money going out |
| capital | starting money |
| disrupt | change how things work |

---

## Structure rules

- Start every section with the most important information — do not build up to the point
- Use numbered lists for steps and ordered processes
- Use bullet points for options or examples (not steps)
- Never use more than 5 bullets or steps without breaking into sub-sections
- Short paragraphs: maximum 4 sentences per paragraph

---

## Tone

- Warm but not childish
- Encouraging without being patronising
- Direct: do not hedge — "you will" not "you might want to try to"
- Practical: everything connects to something real the student can do or has seen
- The teacher sounds like a guide, not an expert lecturer

---

## What "Scandi-level English" means

In Scandinavian countries, students typically learn English from age 6–10 in school and consume a lot of English media. Their English is accurate, simple, and direct — they do not use complex idioms or long sentences. The writing style used in Swedish or Norwegian government communications (clear, no jargon, plain language) is the right reference point.

This is not "dumbed down" English. It is precise, respectful English that does not rely on shared cultural references or native-speaker idioms.
