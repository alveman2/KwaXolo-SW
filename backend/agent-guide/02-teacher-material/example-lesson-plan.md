# Example Teacher Lesson Plan

Topic: D1 — Creating a professional email and using it  
Time available: 45 minutes  
Class context: "Most students use WhatsApp but have never opened Gmail."

---

## LESSON TITLE
How to Set Up and Use a Professional Email

## LEARNING OBJECTIVE
After this lesson, you will be able to create a Gmail account and use it to send a professional message to a customer or employer.

## WRITE ON THE BOARD
- Email is a formal business tool
- Your email address is your online name
- Use your real name — not a nickname
- Subject line tells the reader what it is about
- One email, one topic

## EXPLAIN TO STUDENTS
*(Teacher reads this aloud and explains in their own words)*

Today we are going to talk about email. You already know WhatsApp — you send messages every day. Email is the same idea, but it is more formal. Businesses, schools, and government offices use email. If you apply for a job, the employer will ask for your email. If you sell products and someone asks for a quote or invoice, you send it by email.

The most common email service is Gmail, made by Google. It is free. You can use it on any phone that has internet.

When you create your email address, choose your real name. If your name is Thandi Nzama, your email should be something like `thandi.nzama@gmail.com` or `tnzama@gmail.com`. Do not use a nickname or numbers like `coolgirl2009@gmail.com`. That looks unprofessional to a customer or employer.

When you write an email, always include:
- A subject line — this tells the reader what the email is about before they open it
- A greeting — start with "Dear [Name]" or "Hello [Name]"
- Your message — keep it short and to the point
- A sign-off — end with "Kind regards" and your name

Here is a real example from this area. Samke Jaca runs Inkify, a printing business in the community. When a customer asks for a price on business cards, she does not only reply on WhatsApp — she also sends an email with the price written clearly, so the customer has a record. That looks professional and builds trust.

After this lesson, you will practise creating a Gmail account on your phone and sending a test email.

## DISCUSSION QUESTIONS
1. Have you ever received an email from a school, business, or government office? What was it about?
2. If you run a business, what kind of messages would you send by email that you would not send on WhatsApp?
3. What could go wrong if you use a nickname or unprofessional address like `hotboy99@gmail.com` when applying for a job?

## LOCAL EXAMPLE
Inkify (run by Samke Jaca and Ntokozo Gwacela) is a printing service in the community. They use email to send quotes and invoices to customers, alongside WhatsApp for day-to-day communication. Customers who want a formal record of what they paid prefer email. This is why having a professional email address matters even if you mainly use WhatsApp.

## TIME GUIDE
- 5 min: write board points, students copy
- 15 min: teacher reads and explains the "Explain to Students" section
- 10 min: discussion questions
- 10 min: students begin the student task on their phones
- 5 min: recap — what is one thing you learned today?
